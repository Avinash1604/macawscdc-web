Behaviour Therapy

Behaviour Therapy for Children with Developmental Delays

What is Behaviour Therapy?

Behaviour therapy is a structured, evidence-based approach that helps children learn positive behaviours, communication skills, and daily living abilities. It focuses on understanding how a child behaves, why certain behaviours occur, and how to guide them toward more functional and independent actions.

Why is Behaviour Therapy Needed?

Children with developmental delays may:

  • Struggle to communicate their needs
  • Show repetitive or challenging behaviours
  • Have difficulty with social interaction
  • Find it hard to follow instructions or routines

Behaviour therapy helps bridge these gaps by teaching alternative, appropriate behaviours, reducing frustration-driven actions, and improving learning readiness and independence.

Key Needs Addressed

  1. Communication Skills (verbal and non-verbal expression)
  2. Social Skills (turn-taking, understanding cues)
  3. Behaviour Management (reducing challenging behaviours)
  4. Daily Living Skills (eating, dressing, toileting)
  5. Attention & Learning Readiness

How We Plan Each Session

Each session is structured: pre-session review, warm-up, focused skill teaching, play-based learning, behaviour support, and cool down. Techniques include positive reinforcement, prompting, visual supports, and parent coaching.

Benefits

  • Improved communication and language skills
  • Reduced tantrums and difficult behaviours
  • Enhanced social interaction and play skills
  • Greater independence in daily activities
Session Structure
  • Warm-up and rapport building
  • Skill teaching with reinforcement
  • Play-based generalisation
  • Parent training & home program
