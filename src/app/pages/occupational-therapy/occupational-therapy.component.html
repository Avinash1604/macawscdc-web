Occupational Therapy

Occupational Therapy for Children with Developmental Delays

What is Occupational Therapy (OT)?

Occupational Therapy helps children develop the skills they need for everyday life—playing, learning, socializing, and becoming independent.

Our Approach

  • Child-centered and play-based therapy
  • Evidence-based techniques
  • Individualized care plans
  • Family-focused support

Who Can Benefit?

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • ADHD
  • Global developmental delay
  • Sensory Processing Disorder
  • Learning difficulties

Assessment

  • Developmental history
  • Sensory profile evaluation
  • Motor skill testing
  • Functional skills analysis

Therapy Plan: How It Works

Each child receives an individualized plan based on their needs.

Key Components

  • Goal setting
  • Sensory integration
  • Motor skill training
  • Self-care development

Session Structure

Typically 30–45 minutes based on individual needs.

Parent Involvement

Includes home programs, daily routine guidance, and regular feedback sessions.

Benefits of Occupational Therapy

  • Improved independence
  • Better focus
  • Improved coordination
  • Enhanced confidence

Why Early Intervention Matters

The earlier therapy begins, the better the outcomes.
